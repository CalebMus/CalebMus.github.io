Scientific Philosophy

This should and will evolve as I continue to learn and develop my skills. Currently, and without discussing my specific work, it is rooted in a synthesis of curiosity, pragmatism, and a desire for transformative impact. I approach science as a means of uncovering the "how" and "why" behind complex systems, with a deep respect for knowledge as an intrinsic good. I dislike rigid disciplinary boundaries and believe breakthroughs emerge at the intersections of fields and thoughts. Curiosity should be expansive, but balanced by pragmatic optimism, as science pursues actionable goals with real-world constraints in mind. I believe science should be driven by a child-like wonder, guided by the maturity and rigor of sophisticated methods. And open science is the best!

Artistic Philosophy

Art is everything science is not, the best art inspires the best science (with room for the reverse to be true as well).
